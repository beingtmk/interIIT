Tourist Attraction



Guwahati is situated in the foothills of the Shillong Plateau and blessed by the everflowing mighty Brahmaputra. The earliest mention of this ancient city can be found in the puranas and other religious scriptures. Owing to the time that this city has been around, it has its own rich share of history, culture and cuisine.

The city of Guwahati boasts of the ancient Shakti temple of Goddess Kamakhya, the ancient astrological temple Navagraha, and archaeological remains in Basistha. This region is also home to diverse wildlife including rare and at risk animals like asian elephants, pythons, tigers, rhinoceros, Indian Bison, primate species and endangered birds.

Currently Guwahati is the largest urban area of the north-east and one of the fastest growing cities of India. It is also a major educational and sports hub of the Northeast India. Which has groomed the likes of Hima Das, Shiva Thapa and Jayanta Talukdar.

Umananda Island is the smallest river island in the midst of river Brahmaputra flowing through the city of Guwahati in Assam, a state in northeast India. The British named the island Peacock Island for its structure.

Tourists find it hard to believe that the 3+ km. width of Brahmaputra is one of its narrowest section. Its widest section is over 10 km in width.

The Ahom dynasty, which ruled primarily over Assam for 600 years, was India’s longest unbroken dynasty. .

Although the Kaziranga National Park has the highest number of the great Indian One-Horned Rhinos, the Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary has the highest density meaning you are more likely to spot the majestic beast here.

The Mughals invaded Assam seventeen times, and they were defeated by the handful of Ahoms in Battle of Itakhuli and Battle of Saraighat.

Guwahati has the largest tea auction market in India. The tea here once reached a record breaking price of ₹39,001/kg.

Many ancient Hindu temples are situated in Guwahati, giving it the name "City of Temples".
